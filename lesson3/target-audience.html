Target Audience

The target audience are small business owners and professionals in the Black Forest community. These are individuals who are either native to the Black Forest community or are moving within our city boundaries. Generally, these are either small-to-medium business owners and professionals who seek to take advantage of the network of businesses and professionals that are native to the area, gaining resources from that group and aiding in the betterment of the community as a whole.

Dr. Frank Atkins

Dr. Atkins acted as an optometrist for the people of Dallas for fifteen years before deciding that he needed a change of pace. Having heard many good things about the beautiful country and welcoming people of Black Forest, he closed his Dallas office. Finding an office for rent in Black Forest's city center, he plans to open his new location within the next three months. He knows that he is comin to Black Forest as an unknown, and will need the support of the local Chamber of Commerce to help spread word of mouth and bring in new clients.

Dr. Atkins wants:

  • help getting word of mouth out about his new practice.
  • to be kept aware of future and potential events that might affect his business and the community.
  • a voice in what happens to his local community, including future businesses and developments.

Jimmy

James Morris, 'Jimmy' to his friends, has lived in Black Forest his entire life. Always good with his hands, he was well-known in high school for his beautiful woodworking projects. After years of supplementing his income with projects sold through online retailers, he finally has the confidence to commit to a brick and mortar store. Having secured a business loan, he is hoping to find a new location to set up shop, and to get the help of the local community in supporting his new venture.

Jimmy wants:

  • help navigating the paperwork involved in transitioning his business to a brick and mortar location.
  • understanding the laws and taxes that will affect him as the owner of a physical store.
  • to help plan and participate in community events and activities.
